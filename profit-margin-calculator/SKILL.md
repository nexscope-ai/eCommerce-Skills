# Profit Margin Calculator

Calculate profit margins, break-even points, and pricing strategies for any e-commerce business. Works across all platforms and business models.

## What it does

**Input:** Product costs, selling prices, and business expenses  
**Output:** Detailed profit analysis with margins, break-even points, and pricing recommendations  
**Use case:** Pricing decisions, profitability analysis, financial planning for e-commerce businesses

## Instructions

I'll analyze your product profitability and provide comprehensive financial insights.

### Required Information

Please provide the following details about your product:

1. **Product Costs:**
   - Product cost (wholesale/manufacturing price)
   - Shipping cost to your location
   - Any import duties or taxes

2. **Selling Details:**
   - Current or planned selling price
   - Platform fees (Amazon: 15%, eBay: 10%, Shopify: 2.9%, etc.)
   - Payment processing fees (usually 2.9% + $0.30)

3. **Additional Costs (if applicable):**
   - Advertising costs per unit
   - Storage/warehousing costs
   - Packaging materials
   - Labor costs per unit
   - Return/refund rate (% of sales)

### The Analysis Process

1. **Cost Breakdown:** I'll categorize all your costs (COGS, platform fees, fulfillment, etc.)
2. **Margin Calculations:** Calculate gross margin, net margin, and contribution margin
3. **Break-even Analysis:** Determine minimum selling price and units needed to break even
4. **Pricing Recommendations:** Suggest optimal pricing tiers with profit projections
5. **Scenario Planning:** Show profit at different price points and volume levels

## Pricing Strategy Features

- **Competitive Analysis:** Compare your margins to industry benchmarks
- **Volume Scenarios:** See profit impact of selling 100, 500, 1000+ units
- **Cost Optimization:** Identify which costs have the biggest impact on profitability
- **Risk Assessment:** Analyze sensitivity to cost increases and price decreases
- **ROI Calculations:** Return on investment for marketing spend and inventory

## Business Model Support

✅ **Dropshipping:** No inventory costs, focus on marketing and platform fees  
✅ **Private Label:** Include development, tooling, and inventory costs  
✅ **Wholesale:** Volume pricing and inventory carrying costs  
✅ **Retail Arbitrage:** Quick flip calculations with sourcing costs  
✅ **Handmade/Artisan:** Labor costs and time-based pricing  
✅ **Digital Products:** Low marginal costs, high-margin analysis

## Platform-Specific Calculations

- **Amazon:** FBA fees, storage costs, PPC advertising
- **Shopify:** Transaction fees, app costs, marketing attribution  
- **eBay:** Final value fees, promoted listings, shipping
- **Etsy:** Transaction and payment fees, advertising costs
- **WooCommerce:** Payment gateway, hosting, marketing costs
- **Facebook/Instagram Shop:** Social media advertising costs

## Limitations

- Requires accurate cost data for meaningful results
- Cannot predict market demand or competitive response
- Fixed costs (like monthly subscriptions) need to be allocated per unit
- Seasonal variations in costs or demand are not automatically factored in
- Tax implications vary by location and should be consulted with accountant

## Best Practices

1. **Be Conservative:** Slightly overestimate costs to avoid surprises
2. **Track Actual Costs:** Compare predictions to real performance monthly
3. **Factor Growth:** Higher volumes often reduce per-unit costs
4. **Consider Seasonality:** Holiday spikes, inventory clearance impacts
5. **Test Price Points:** Use data to validate pricing assumptions
